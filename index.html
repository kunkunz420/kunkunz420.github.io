Core Research

Institutional Common Ownership and Executive Mobility

Working Paper

Supervisor: Prof. Jose Azar

This paper investigates institutional common ownership as an informal non-compete mechanism in executive labor markets. I execute a Difference-in-Differences design leveraging the 2009 BlackRock–BGI merger as a quasi-experimental shock to common ownership structures. The analysis quantifies competitive effects through MHHI Delta metrics implemented in Stata, examining how overlapping institutional ownership among industry peers reduces cross-firm executive mobility.

Open Source Projects

ANDV-Behavioral-SEIRQ

GitHub

Andes Virus Epidemiological-Economic Model — a sophisticated, multi-language pipeline integrating behavioral economics with SEIRQ epidemiological dynamics to model the transmission of the Andes Virus (ANDV) under endogenous human behavioral responses.

Behavioral-Economic Core. The model extends the canonical SEIRQ framework by incorporating a behavioral feedback loop: agents adjust their social contact rates in response to perceived infection risk, prevalence information, and economic constraints. This micro-founded approach captures the two-way interaction between epidemic dynamics and human decision-making — a feature absent from purely epidemiological models.

Multi-Language Implementation. The pipeline is engineered across Python, R, Stata, and MATLAB, enabling seamless integration with existing econometric workflows. Parameter estimation routines leverage maximum likelihood and Bayesian methods across languages, while sensitivity analyses and counterfactual simulations are parallelized for computational efficiency.

Advanced Parameter Extraction. The model implements sophisticated calibration techniques for the basic reproduction number (R0), behavioral response elasticities, and quarantine effectiveness parameters. Real-world ANDV incidence data from public health repositories is harmonized with economic time series to estimate structural parameters and evaluate policy counterfactuals under various behavioral scenarios.
