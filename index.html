Research

“Institutional Common Ownership and Executive Mobility”

Supervisor: Prof. José Azar (IESE & UNAV) · Working Paper · Download CV (PDF) ↓

Exploiting the BlackRock-BGI merger (2009) as a quasi-natural experiment, I examine how institutional common ownership shapes C-suite executive mobility. Using difference-in-differences and event study designs on a 27.8-million-row panel linking 13F institutional holdings (58.9M observations), ExecuComp executive data (31,959 executive-years), and Orbis director moves (24,716 transitions), I find that common ownership increases mobility by facilitating within-network labor market channels — rejecting the shadow non-compete hypothesis. Robust to 17 pair-level checks, placebo tests, and high-dimensional fixed effects.
