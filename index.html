Working Paper

Institutional Common Ownership and Executive Mobility: Evidence from the 2009 BlackRock–BGI Merger

Working Paper

Supervisor: Prof. José Azar (IESE & UNAV)

  • Exploiting the BlackRock–BGI merger (2009) as a quasi-natural experiment, I examine how institutional common ownership shapes C-suite executive mobility.
  • Constructed a 27.8-million-row firm-pair panel linking 13F holdings (58.9M obs.), ExecuComp data (31,959 executive-years), and Orbis director moves (24,716 transitions).
  • Findings: common ownership increases mobility through within-network labor market channels—rejecting the shadow non-compete hypothesis.
  • Results: Firm-level DiD +0.073 (p = 0.002); pair-level logit Δλ = +0.582 (p < 10⁻¹⁸); event study post-2009 significant (p < 0.001). Robust to 17 pair-level checks and 5 placebo tests.
