## RESEARCH & WORK EXPERIENCE

**University of Navarra** — Pamplona, Spain  
*Working Paper: "Institutional Common Ownership and Executive Mobility"* — Feb. 2026 – Jun. 2026
- Investigated whether institutional common ownership acts as an informal non-compete mechanism in C-suite labor markets.
- Constructed a 27.8-million-row firm-pair panel linking 13F institutional holdings (58.9M observations), S&P ExecuComp executive data (31,959 executive-years), and Orbis director transitions (24,716 moves).
- Implemented Difference-in-Differences leveraging the 2009 BlackRock-BGI merger as a quasi-natural experiment.
- Extended to firm-pair-level Event Study (2005–2020, 16 years) with Origin×Year + Dest×Year high-dimensional fixed effects via FWL absorption.
- Quantified common ownership via $\lambda_{jk}$ (MHHI Delta) and $\Delta\lambda_{jk}$ treatment variables.
- Key result: Common ownership *increases* mobility (firm-level: +0.073, p=0.002; pair-level logit: +0.582, p<10⁻¹⁸).
- Robustness: 17 pair-level checks (15/17 significant), 5 placebo cutoff dates (all p>0.42), time-split validation (Pre-2010: p=0.063; Post-2009: p<0.001).
- Repository: github.com/kunkunz420/common-ownership-executive-mobility
