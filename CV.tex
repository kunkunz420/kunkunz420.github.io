\section{Working Paper}

{\bfseries\itshape``Institutional Common Ownership and Executive Mobility''}\\[2pt]
{\small Supervisor: Prof.\ Jos\'e Azar (IESE \& UNAV)}\\[4pt]
{\small
Exploiting the BlackRock--BGI merger (2009) as a quasi-natural experiment,
I examine how institutional common ownership shapes C-suite executive mobility.
Using difference-in-differences and event study designs on a
\textbf{27.8-million-row firm-pair panel} linking 13F institutional holdings
(58.9M obs.), ExecuComp data (31,959 executive-years), and Orbis director
moves (24,716 transitions), I find that common ownership \emph{increases}
mobility through within-network labor market channels---rejecting the
shadow non-compete hypothesis. Robust across 17 pair-level checks, 5 placebo
tests, and high-dimensional fixed effects.
}
