\section{Working Paper}

{\bfseries\itshape``Institutional Common Ownership and Executive Mobility''}\\[2pt]
{\small Supervisor: Prof.\ Jos\'e Azar (IESE \& UNAV)}\\[4pt]
{\small\color{mid}
Exploiting the BlackRock--BGI merger (2009) as a quasi-natural experiment,
I examine how institutional common ownership shapes C-suite executive mobility.
Using difference-in-differences and event study designs on a
\textbf{27.8-million-row panel} linking 13F institutional holdings (58.9M obs.),
ExecuComp executive data (31,959 executive-years), and Orbis director moves
(24,716 transitions), I find that common ownership \emph{increases} mobility
by facilitating within-network labor market channels---rejecting the shadow
non-compete hypothesis. Robust across 17 pair-level checks, placebo tests,
and high-dimensional fixed effects.
}
