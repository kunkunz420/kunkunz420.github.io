\section{Working Paper}

{\bfseries\itshape``Institutional Common Ownership and Executive Mobility''}\\[1pt]
{\small Supervisor: Prof.\ Jos\'e Azar (IESE \& UNAV)}\\[2pt]
{\small
Exploiting the BlackRock--BGI merger (2009) as a quasi-natural experiment, I examine
how institutional common ownership shapes C-suite executive mobility. Using
difference-in-differences and event study designs on a \textbf{27.8-million-row
firm-pair panel} linking 13F holdings (58.9M obs.), ExecuComp data (31,959
executive-years), and Orbis director moves (24,716 transitions), I find that
common ownership \emph{increases} mobility through within-network labor market
channels---rejecting the shadow non-compete hypothesis.\\
\textbf{Results:} Firm-level DiD: +0.073 ($p=0.002$). Pair-level logit:
$\Delta\lambda = +0.582$ ($p<10^{-18}$). Event study: Post-2009 significant
($p<0.001$). Robust to 17 pair-level checks, 5 placebo tests.\\
\href{https://github.com/kunkunz420/common-ownership-executive-mobility}{github.com/kunkunz420/common-ownership-executive-mobility}
}
