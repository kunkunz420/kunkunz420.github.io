\section{Research}

\textbf{Working Paper}\\[2pt]
{\bfseries ``Institutional Common Ownership and Executive Mobility''}\\
\textit{Supervisor: Prof.\ José Azar (IESE \& UNAV)}\\[2pt]
\small
Exploiting the BlackRock-BGI merger (2009) as a quasi-natural experiment, I examine how
institutional common ownership shapes C-suite executive mobility. Using difference-in-differences
and event study designs on a 27.8-million-row panel linking 13F institutional holdings
(58.9M observations), ExecuComp executive data (31,959 executive-years), and Orbis director
moves (24,716 transitions), I find that common ownership \textit{increases} mobility by
facilitating within-network labor market channels---rejecting the shadow non-compete hypothesis.
Robust to 17 pair-level checks, placebo tests, and high-dimensional fixed effects.
